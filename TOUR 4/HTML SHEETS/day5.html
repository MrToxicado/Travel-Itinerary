Events And Festivals

The vibrant city of Vienna is host to a great range of annual events and festivals, with everything from jazz festivals, to light shows, to film festivals. Whether you are in the city to sight-see over the weekend or on a trip for business, plan your stay right to make sure it coincides with one of these unique festivals in Berlin.

Life Ball Festival

Vienna's lifeball is a surprising success story. It was started in 1993 in Vienna as an AIDS charity event and quickly became the biggest, most color- and successful event of its sort throughout Europe.The ball's aim is rather obvious: fund raising to support various AIDS related activities from preventation to counselling and direct aid to HIV positive people.






Donauiensel

Open-air festival 'Donauinselfest' has taken place over 20 time and is already a favourite among locals and visitors. The festival is organised once a year and spreads all over the popular recreational mecca Danube Island. It attracts up to 3 million visitors with free entry and international stars. The island can easily accommodate this amount of visitors: More than 19 stages ('isles') offer entertainment from pop, rock, jazz to hip hop. Don't miss it!







The Film Festival

Vienna's major film festival Viennale took place for the first time in 1960. Since then it has aimed to present international and Austrian films of high-quality and is proud of its international reputation.Venues are spread over Vienna's historic centre. Usually, about 200 films, documentaries and short films are broadcast and attract more than 70,000 visitors!





New Years Eve

At midnight you will hear famous Pummerin (St. Stephen’s Cathedral) strike! Apart from that, everything can happen during the biggest celebration of the year. You can opt for an open-air party inclusive last minute waltz course all over Vienna’s first district (‘Silvesterpfad’), escape three-four time at various clubs.Just before midnight you can watch the official fireworks, which are supported by considerable private efforts, illuminate the city!

Giant Ferris Wheel

Visiting the Prater, a large natural park between the Danube and the Danube Canal, is a little like stepping into another world.A highlight for sightseers is taking a ride on the famous Giant Wheel,landmark that has provided views over the city.

Veinna State Opera

One of the world's largest and most splendid theaters, the Vienna State Opera House (Wiener Staatsoper) has hosted many of the world's most prominent composers, conductors, soloists, and dancers.

Burg Theatre

The National Theater, has been famous for its productions of German-language plays. After devastation by bombing and fire in 1945, the theater eventually reopened in 1955 and has since grown in stature as the country's most important theater.
