Events And Festivals

The vibrant city of Berlin is host to a great range of annual events and festivals, with everything from jazz festivals, to light shows, to film festivals. Whether you are in the city to sight-see over the weekend or on a trip for business, plan your stay right to make sure it coincides with one of these unique festivals in Berlin.

Festival of Lights

Every year, millions of visitors flock to Berlin to see the city lit by amazing illuminations. The city is majestically transformed as artistic lights are placed around landmark architecture and venues for the Festival of Lights (Berlin Leuchtet). The festival aims to tell stories and draw attention to unique, hidden and essential spots in Berlin. The Festival of Lights is one of the largest festivals of its kind in the world.

Since 2015, the World Championship of projection mapping has been a permanent feature. Head to the Kohlhoff Tower at the Potsdamer Platz to witness some of the world's most talented video mapping artists compete against each other.






JazzFest

Towards the end of autumn, the city of Berlin turns in to a hub for fans of jazz from around the world. For 44 years the Jazzfest Berlin (Berlin Jazz Festival) has been held in a variety of locations around the city. It showcases the diverse talents of international jazz musicians and ensembles. The popular Jazzfest Berlin takes place from November 5 to November 8 and this year it will go partly off-stage, with artists and cultural workers sharing online projects.







Berlinale

The Berlin International Film Festival, also known as the Berlinale, has become one of the most important international film festivals, second after Cannes'. Taking place in February every year, it features over 400 films, has sold over 335,000 tickets, and welcomes nearly 20,000 professional visitors from 127 countries. Access the full programme as of January, and plan ahead for your next stay at Radisson Blu Berlin as this is a very popular event.





Carnival Of Cultures

The diversity of Berlin is celebrated every May at the Karneval der Kulturen (Carnival of Cultures). This 4 day urban festival shows the many faces of Berlin with its free and open programme of events. Throughout the Carnival, visitors can celebrate cultural and ethnic diversity with a range of events around Blücherplatz. There are hundreds of market stalls with tasty food, traditional craft, art and gifts, local and international bands performing, and street performers showing their skill.

Sealife Berlin

The Aquarium Berlin in Berlin is one of Germany's largest aquariums. The aquarium was built in 1913 as part of the Berlin Zoological Garden complex.The Aquarium has been ranked among the public aquariums with the world’s greatest biodiversity.
